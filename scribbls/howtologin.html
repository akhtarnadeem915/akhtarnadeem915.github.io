How to log-in securely

Posted by Nadeem akhtar

You may have heard by people saying consider changing your password every once in a while to be more secure, but does it really work, well turns out it doesn’t, no matter how strong your password is or how often you change it, you are still not secure......, if you feel like vulnerable then read this blog till end.

Your username and password are “shared secret”, they are stored on servers if not then you probably will never be able to sign-in again with your previous username and password, the saved password could be stolen from the server, or you could be a victim of phishing attack where you could lose all of your credentials.

A 2017 stats, report 1 in 131 emails sent are malicious they contain malware not only ransomware but other too, so how to securely sign-in.

well as per google you should probably use a second factor authentication, such as physical security tokens, there are the two types of physical security tokens U2F and UAF, but we already use a second-factor authentication such as OTP then why should buy a physical token instead?

the answer is that OTP’s have some real-time vulnerability issues such as MITM and MITB attacks, this attacks can interfere with your network and can get access of your OTP, so using a physical security token is the best idea, the mere standard is backed by the FIDO Alliance, which includes Google, Microsoft, PayPal, Visa, Samsung, and many other massive companies.

FIDO Alliance

U2F and UAF keys are only supported by limited web browsers such as chrome, edge, firefox, w3c, this devices are usually small USB devices that you can insert in your computer’s USB port, some of them have NFC support so they can be used with android phones but If you have an iOS device you cannot use these because of the nifty NFC on the inside that allows you to interface with android device doesn’t work the way that Apple implements NFC on an iPhone so you could use a Bluetooth authentication token on any iOS device instead, whereas UAF keys provide you a passwordless experience means it uses biometrics such as fingerprints.
